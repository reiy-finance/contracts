## Background

Since review 004 the reward/fee mechanism was rewritten to a CoW-Protocol-style model:

- **Fees** moved from a single BPS `protocol_fee` collected as a `Coin<N>` at `close_batch` to a
  per-settlement **PPM** split out of the buy coin (`compute_fees`: volume fee by pair tier + surplus
  fee `min(50% surplus, 0.98% gross)`, total capped at 1% gross), deposited into a per-token
  `FeeVault<T>` pinned to `GlobalConfig`.
- **Solver rewards** moved from a treasury-funded pro-rata split to a **VCG per-solver** reward at
  `close_batch`: `reward_i = clamp(actual_total_score − referenceScore_i, 0, β × fee_i)`, where
  `referenceScore_i` (`auction::reference_score_excluding`) is the best score achievable without
  solver `i`, `fee_i` is the protocol fee from `i`'s settled intents, and `β =
  solver_reward_fee_share_ppm` (default 50%).
- `close_batch` signature changed: it now takes `FeeVault<N>` (not a fee coin / treasury) and
  distributes rewards. `trigger_fallback` is unchanged and still routes slashed stake to the treasury.
- A `max_allocations` config cap was added and asserted in `submit_allocation`.

This is the first review of that rewrite.

## [Medium] Reward suppression via undeliverable "paper" alternative allocation

**Severity:** Medium &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** Tampering / Repudiation

**Location:** `sources/auction.move:1173` (`reference_score_excluding`), `:1195`
(`allocation_is_valid` gate); consumed in `sources/settlement.move:415`
(`distribute_solver_rewards`).

**Description.** `referenceScore_i` is computed as the max score over allocations that (a) contain no
bids from solver `i` and (b) pass `allocation_is_valid`. `allocation_is_valid` only checks a *static*
condition — each constituent bid's declared `payout ≥ floor = max(m_eff, benchmark_payout)` and that
the declared score sums correctly. It does **not** require that the alternative allocation was, or
could be, actually settled.

A competing solver can therefore submit a bid + allocation whose payouts clear the floor on paper,
scoring just below the winning allocation, with **no intention of ever settling it** (it will not be
selected as winner, so it is never required to deliver). That valid-but-undeliverable allocation
raises `referenceScore_i` for the genuine winner, shrinking `marginal = actual_score −
referenceScore_i` toward zero and suppressing the winner's reward. The griefer's only cost is the
`required_allocation_stake` reservation, which is **released, not slashed**, when its allocation does
not win — i.e. ~free (one epoch of locked stake).

**Impact.** The core new incentive (VCG performance reward) can be cheaply degraded by a rival who
need not prove deliverability. Suppressed rewards reduce the payoff for honest winning solvers →
weakens participation incentive and, downstream, settlement liveness. No principal or protocol funds
are at risk, but the reward mechanism's fairness — the reason for adopting VCG — is undermined. This
is Reiy's analogue of the "fairness filtering" problem CoW's combinatorial auction explicitly guards
against; Reiy has no such filter.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** Make the counterfactual cost something. Options, lowest-risk first:
(a) only count an alternative allocation in `referenceScore_i` if its constituent bids carried a
*non-trivial committed stake* (`grief_factor × score`) — i.e. the alternative is backed by a slashable
bond, so a never-delivered reference costs the griefer; (b) restrict the reference to allocations the
auctioneer actually proposed as selection candidates that *survived* validity at selection time and
record them once at commit (avoids accepting late paper allocations); (c) document that reference is
"best validatable counterfactual" and accept the conservative (winner-under-rewarding) bias. Tests:
winner reward with vs without a planted sub-winning valid allocation from a third solver.

## [Medium] `close_batch` reward loop is O(solvers × allocations × bids × intents)

**Severity:** Medium &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** Denial of Service

**Location:** `sources/settlement.move:415` (`distribute_solver_rewards` loop over winning solvers),
calling `sources/auction.move:1173` (`reference_score_excluding`), which calls `allocation_is_valid`
(`:867`, O(bids × intents)) for **every allocation, for every solver**.

**Description.** `distribute_solver_rewards` iterates winning solvers; for each it calls
`reference_score_excluding`, which re-runs `allocation_is_valid` across all allocations. Validity is
independent of which solver is excluded, so it is recomputed redundantly `S` times. Worst-case cost is
`O(S × A × B × I)` where `A ≤ max_allocations` (default **64**), `B ≤ max_allocation_bids` (32),
`I ≤ max_allocation_intents` (128), and `S` is bounded by the winning-solver count. At the default
caps this is ~`64 × 32 × 128 ≈ 2.6 × 10⁵` validity-iterations **per solver**, multiplied by `S`.

The `max_allocations` cap (this change) converts the previously *unbounded* `close_batch` loop
(001/004 Medium) into a *bounded* one — a real improvement — but the bound, combined with the
per-solver recompute, is large enough that a fully-packed epoch could still approach the
transaction computation budget, re-introducing the "epoch cannot be closed" liveness risk that the cap
was meant to remove.

**Impact.** Liveness: a maliciously dense (but valid) allocation set could make `close_batch` too
expensive to execute, stranding the epoch in Settlement. Lower severity than the original unbounded
finding because it is now bounded and tunable, but not eliminated.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** (1) Precompute `(valid, score, solver_set)` per allocation **once** at
the top of `distribute_solver_rewards`, then make `reference_score_excluding` O(A) per solver
(membership + max) instead of re-validating — drops cost to `O(A×B×I + S×A)`. (2) Lower the
`max_allocations` default (e.g. 16–32) until the precompute lands. (3) Add a gas/iteration regression
test at the configured caps. Security-first framing: until (1), treat the default cap as a liveness
parameter, not just a hygiene bound.

## [Low] `FeeTier::Custom(ppm)` is unvalidated

**Severity:** Low &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** Denial of Service / Tampering

**Location:** `sources/config.move:333` (`set_pair_fee_tier`), `:352` (`volume_fee_ppm_for_pair`,
`Custom(ppm) => ppm`); consumed in `sources/settlement.move:221` (`compute_fees`).

**Description.** `set_standard_volume_fee_ppm` / `set_correlated_volume_fee_ppm` both assert
`v ≤ MAX_VOLUME_FEE_PPM (10_000) && v ≤ max_total_fee_ppm`. The per-pair `FeeTier::Custom(ppm)` path
bypasses this entirely — `set_pair_fee_tier` stores the tier with no bound check, and
`volume_fee_ppm_for_pair` returns the raw `ppm`. If an admin sets `Custom(ppm)` with `ppm > 1_000_000`,
`compute_fees` computes `volume_fee = gross × ppm / 1e6 > gross`, and the subsequent
`gross − volume_fee` underflows (u64) → **every settlement on that pair aborts** (pair bricked). Even
`ppm` between `MAX_VOLUME_FEE_PPM` and `1e6` silently exceeds the intended fee ceiling enforced
everywhere else.

**Impact.** AdminCap-gated, so an operational footgun rather than an external exploit — but it defeats
the fee-ceiling invariant the other setters uphold and can DoS a trading pair via a single
mis-set parameter. Matters more as admin power migrates toward governance/multisig (decentralization
priority), where param validation is the only guardrail.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** In `set_pair_fee_tier`, when the tier is `Custom(ppm)`, assert
`ppm ≤ MAX_VOLUME_FEE_PPM && ppm ≤ c.max_total_fee_ppm` (same bound as the global setters). Test:
`set_pair_fee_tier(Custom(10_001))` aborts `EInvalidParam`.

## [Low] AdminCap fee withdrawal can silently starve pending rewards

**Severity:** Low &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** Repudiation / Denial of Service

**Location:** `sources/fee_vault.move` (`withdraw_fees`, AdminCap), interaction with
`sources/settlement.move:444` (`distribute_solver_rewards` per-solver `fee_vault::balance ≥
solver_reward` guard).

**Description.** Fees are deposited into the vault during settlement; rewards are paid at `close_batch`.
Between those two points an `AdminCap` holder can `withdraw_fees` and drop the vault below the rewards
owed for the epoch. `distribute_solver_rewards` guards each payout with
`fee_vault::balance(fee_vault) ≥ solver_reward` and simply **skips** (no abort, no event) a solver it
cannot pay. A legitimately-earned reward then silently vanishes with no on-chain signal.

**Impact.** Conditional on AdminCap behaviour (trusted-ish today) but: (a) it is a path by which earned
rewards are withheld without trace, a repudiation/fairness concern; (b) the silent-skip means even an
*accidental* admin withdrawal degrades solver payouts invisibly. Budget-safety itself is intact — the
per-solver `β × fee_i` cap guarantees `Σ reward ≤ fees deposited this epoch`, so the vault can only be
short if value was withdrawn, never by over-distribution.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** Either (a) reserve the epoch's reward budget so `withdraw_fees` cannot
draw it down before `close_batch`, or (b) emit a distinct `SolverRewardSkipped` event (with reason)
when the balance guard trips so the omission is observable, and document the settlement→close window as
a period during which fee withdrawal must not run. Prefer (a) for a complete product.

## [Informational] `max_ucp_rounding_loss` is dead config

**Severity:** Informational &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** —

**Location:** `sources/config.move:103` (field), `:150` (default), `:270` (setter), `:483` (getter).

**Description.** `max_ucp_rounding_loss` is declared, defaulted, settable (AdminCap), and readable, but
**never consumed** — the UCP equality check (`record_settlement` cross-ratio) is exact and the

`auctioneer_share_bps` finding removed this round.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** Either wire it into a tolerance on the UCP/k comparison (if a bounded
rounding slack is intended) or remove it to avoid an inactive-control footgun.

## [Informational] Tautological `EBelowMinimum` guard; `EBelowMinimum` unreachable on production path

**Severity:** Informational &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Category (STRIDE):** —

**Location:** `sources/settlement.move:306` (`assert!(gross >= net + total_fee, EBelowMinimum)`),
`:33` (error decl), `:583` (only live use, in the test-only settle path).

**Description.** In `finalize_settlement`, `net = gross − total_fee`, so `gross ≥ net + total_fee` is
identically true — the assert can never fire and documents an invariant rather than checking one. On
the production path (`settle_intent_numeraire` / `settle_intent`), the `m_eff` floor is enforced
transitively (`compute_fees` asserts `payout_after_volume ≥ protected_min ≥ m_eff` → `EBelowFloor`), so
`EBelowMinimum` is only reachable from the `#[test_only]` `settle_intent_with_values_for_testing`
(`:583`). Not a vulnerability; a clarity/dead-code note.

**Recommendation (hướng sửa).** Drop the tautological assert (or replace with a comment), and note that
`EBelowMinimum` is a test-path-only error so future readers don't assume it gates production.

## Re-verified OK (no change required this round)

- **Reward budget-safety holds.** The per-solver cap `reward_i ≤ β × fee_i` with `β ≤ 1e6` and
  `Σ fee_i` equal to the fees deposited this epoch gives `Σ reward_i ≤ Σ fee_i ≤ vault balance`; the
  per-payout `balance ≥ reward` guard and `pay_reward`'s own `amount ≤ balance` assert are belt-and-
  suspenders. The vault cannot underflow via distribution.
- **No double-distribute / double-close.** `close_batch` asserts Settlement phase up front and calls
  `set_closed` at the end; a second call aborts before re-distributing. `close_batch` and
  `trigger_fallback` remain mutually exclusive (004 re-confirmed).
- **Fee rounding is conservative.** All fees use `mul_div_floor` (protocol collects ≤ exact → user-
  favourable); rewards use `mul_div_floor` (solver receives ≤ exact → protocol-favourable). No
  rounding-direction griefing.
- **`compute_fees` preserves `net ≥ protected_min`.** `surplus_fee ≤ surplus_after_volume` for
  `share_ppm ≤ 1e6`, so `net = payout_after_volume − surplus_fee ≥ protected_min`; total cap only
  lowers the fee. User always receives ≥ `max(m_eff, floor)`.
- **Arithmetic overflow-safe.** `math::mul_div_floor/ceil` use `u128` intermediates with a `to_u64`
  `EOverflow` guard; PPM math (`gross × ppm / 1e6`) cannot overflow `u128` for `u64` inputs.
- **Per-epoch state reset is complete.** `solver_fee_collected` is reset in `start_new_epoch` alongside
  `solver_actual_score`; the removed `batch_total_fee_collected` / `committed_benchmark_score` fields
  leave no dangling reset. No cross-epoch fee/score leak.
- **Fee-cap economic incentive is sound.** Inflating one's own `fee_i` to raise the reward cap requires
  sourcing a larger gross payout; the extra reward (`≤ β × feerate × ΔG`) is far smaller than the extra
  cost `ΔG`, so over-paying to farm rewards is unprofitable.
- **`FeeVault<N>` canonical binding** is asserted in `close_batch` and every `settle_*` — closes the
  004 object-binding gap *for the fee path* (registry/treasury binding still Open from 004).

## Methodology

STRIDE applied per public/package function across the changed surface: fee computation
(`compute_fees`), vault custody (`fee_vault`: deposit/withdraw/pay_reward/assert_canonical), reward
distribution (`distribute_solver_rewards`, `reference_score_excluding`), and the `close_batch` /
`submit_allocation` deltas. Each was checked for authorization & canonical-object binding, balance
conservation (buy-token fee split, vault, reward budget), rounding direction, overflow boundaries,
loop bounds (DoS), reward griefing / reference manipulation, per-epoch state reset, and
double-spend/double-distribute across close vs fallback. Findings above are deltas from "defended".
